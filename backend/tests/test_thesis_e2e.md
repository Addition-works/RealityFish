# Mobile Vibe Coding Adoption

## Research Question
How are non-technical entrepreneurs and AI hobbyists in the US currently discovering and using mobile AI coding tools, and what barriers prevent wider adoption?

## Scope
- Platforms: x, reddit
- Keywords: vibe coding, AI app builder, Replit mobile, no-code AI
- Geography: US
- Time Window: 14 days

## Audience Profiles

### AI Hobbyists
People who experiment with AI tools recreationally. They try new apps early, share results, and participate in online communities about AI.
- Behaviors: sharing project screenshots, reviewing AI tools, posting in r/artificial and AI Twitter
- Interests: artificial intelligence, coding, tech gadgets, prompt engineering
- Demographics: 18-40, tech-savvy, early adopters

### Small Business Owners
People running small businesses (restaurants, Etsy shops, local services) who solve problems with manual processes. They may have heard of AI but haven't tried coding tools.
- Behaviors: asking for tool recommendations, sharing business milestones, complaining about tech complexity
- Interests: small business, entrepreneurship, automation, efficiency
- Demographics: 28-55, non-technical, US-based

## Known Context
Google AI Studio is a free platform for building AI-powered applications. A mobile version is forthcoming. Key competitors in mobile vibe coding include Replit Mobile, Rork, and Vibe Studio. Claude Code from Anthropic is the current market leader in AI-assisted coding (desktop). The term "vibe coding" was coined by Andrej Karpathy in early 2025.
